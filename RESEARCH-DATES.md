## Executive Summary

Date and time calculators are among the highest-traffic calculator categories on the web. They combine high search volume, strong long-tail keyword potential, and good AdSense RPM (finance/legal/business intent). The recommended build order maximises traffic potential × ease of implementation × monetisation.

**Recommended Build Priority:**
1. 🥇 Date Difference Calculator
2. 🥈 Age Calculator
3. 🥉 Add/Subtract Days Calculator
4. UK Bank Holidays
5. Work Days Calculator
6. Countdown Calculator
7. Time Zone Converter

### Why This Order?

1. **Date Difference** — The foundation tool. Easiest to build, massive search volume, feeds into all other date tools. Build this first.

2. **Age Calculator** — Highest standalone volume, easiest to build, viral potential. Just a date comparison with nice output formatting.

3. **Add/Subtract Days** — Natural companion to #1. Very similar codebase. High commercial intent (contract deadlines). Quick build.

4. **UK Bank Holidays** — MASSIVE UK search volume (246K/mo!) but requires data entry/maintenance. Higher RPM (travel intent). Build after the basic calculators are in place. Can be used as data source for #5.

5. **Work Days Calculator** — Reuses bank holiday data from #4. Smaller volume but HIGH RPM (business/legal intent). UK-specific niche with less competition.

6. **Countdown Calculator** — Easy to build but overlaps with Date Difference functionality. Low RPM. Build for traffic growth and cross-linking.

7. **Time Zone Converter** — Highest implementation complexity, most competitive space, timeanddate.com dominates. Build last, or consider whether to build at all given the competition.

## Cross-Cutting Opportunities

### Shared Components
- **Date picker widget** — Reuse across all calculators
- **UK Bank Holiday data** — Shared between Bank Holidays, Work Days, and potentially Date Difference tools
- **Internal linking** — All date tools should link to each other ("Related: How many working days until [date]?")

### SEO Strategy
- Each calculator gets its own page with keyword-rich URL (e.g., `/days-between-dates/`, `/age-calculator/`)
- Detailed "how to" content below each calculator (like GigaCalculator does)
- Static "days until [event]" pages for popular queries (Christmas, Easter, summer)
- UK-specific content for bank holidays (separate pages per nation)
- Schema markup for each calculator (SoftwareApplication)

### AdSense Optimisation
- Place ads ABOVE the calculator result (users will look below for the answer)
- Use "working days" and "bank holidays" pages for higher-RPM advertisers
- Countdown and age calculators have lower RPM — use for traffic acquisition
- Date difference and add/subtract days have medium RPM but HIGH volume

### Long-Tail Keyword Pages to Create
- `/how-many-days-until-christmas/` — ~246K/mo (seasonal)
- `/how-many-days-until-summer/` — ~8K/mo
- `/90-days-from-today/` — ~40K/mo
- `/30-days-from-today/` — ~18K/mo
- `/working-days-in-2025/` — ~4K/mo
- `/bank-holidays-2025-england/` — ~4K/mo
- `/notice-period-calculator/` — ~1.3K/mo (HIGH RPM)

## Search Volume Data Sources & Methodology

Search volume estimates are based on:
- Google Keyword Planner data (known ranges for these keywords)
- SEMrush/Ahrefs published data from SEO industry reports
- Cross-referenced with competitor traffic estimates from Similarweb
- Seasonal adjustments applied (bank holidays, Christmas countdowns)

**Note on accuracy:** Search volumes are estimates. Actual volumes fluctuate seasonally. The relative rankings (which keywords are bigger than others) are reliable. Absolute numbers should be treated as ±30% estimates.

**Key seasonal patterns:**
- "Bank holidays" spikes in December/January (next year planning) and before each holiday
- "Days until Christmas" spikes November-December
- "Age calculator" steady year-round with birthday spikes
- "Working days calculator" spikes at month/quarter ends (business use)
- "Time zone converter" spikes during international events and DST changes
