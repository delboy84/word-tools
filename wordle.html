How to Use This Wordle Solver

  1. Enter your first guess result Type the word you guessed and mark each letter as green (correct position), yellow (wrong position), or grey (not in the word).
  2. See suggested words The solver shows the best next guesses, ranked by how much they narrow down the remaining possibilities.
  3. Keep entering results After each guess, the solver updates its suggestions based on all the information you've gathered.
  4. Use hard mode Toggle hard mode on if you're playing with the constraint that revealed letters must be used in subsequent guesses.

Use Cases

Solving the daily Wordle. Whether you're stuck on guess 3 or want to start with the optimal opening word, the solver helps you find the answer efficiently. It uses information theory to suggest words that maximise the information gained from each guess.

Improving your Wordle strategy. The solver reveals optimal opening words and shows why certain guesses are better than others. Over time, you'll develop better strategies, like choosing words that test common vowels early.

Hard mode assistance. Hard mode (where you must use revealed letters) makes Wordle significantly more challenging. The solver accounts for this constraint and still finds the optimal path to the answer.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best Wordle opening word?

SALET, TRACE, and CRANE are among the most efficient opening words according to information theory. They test common letters (E, A, T, R, S, L) in positions where they're statistically most likely to appear. The solver will suggest these or similar words if you want the mathematical optimal start.

Does it work with Wordle variants?

The solver uses the official Wordle answer list plus the extended ENABLE dictionary, so it works for the original Wordle and most variants. For Quordle, Dordle, or other multi-word variants, you can use the solver for each word separately.

How does hard mode affect the solver?

In hard mode, you must reuse any letters you've already found (green or yellow). This constrains the solver's suggestions. it will only recommend words that include your revealed letters. The solver adapts its strategy automatically when hard mode is enabled.

Does it work offline?

Yes. the entire word list is cached in your browser. You can solve Wordles on the train, on a plane, or anywhere without internet.

Is this cheating?

That's between you and your conscience. Many people use it to learn better strategies, to help when they're stuck on guess 5, or just to satisfy curiosity about optimal play. We won't tell.

It uses information theory to suggest optimal guesses. Not just any valid word, but the word that will narrow down the most possibilities. It works offline, supports hard mode, and learns from every guess you enter. No ads, no tracking, no "download our app" pop-ups.
