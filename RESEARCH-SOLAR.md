#### Key Features Users Expect

- 10-25 year ROI projection

#### Battery Financial Logic (UK 2026)
- Without battery: self-consume 30-50% of solar, export 50-70%
- With battery: self-consume 70-90% of solar, export 10-30%
- Each kWh stored and used saves ~24.5p (import price) minus what you'd have earned exporting (~12p) = **net benefit ~12.5p/kWh**
- Typical battery stores 2,500-4,000 kWh/year more self-consumption
- Annual benefit: £300-£500/year
- Payback on £5,000-£6,000 battery: **10-15 years** (without time-of-use arbitrage)
- With Octopus Flux time-shifting: payback can improve to **7-10 years**

#### Market Gap 💡
- **No simple standalone battery ROI calculator** — it's always bundled with solar ROI
- **Time-of-use arbitrage** (charge cheap at night, use at peak) is RARELY modelled — this is the key value proposition for battery owners on Octopus Go/Flux
- Most tools don't show **"with battery vs without"** side-by-side comparison
- This is a **high-value differentiator** because it directly answers the hardest purchase decision

### 3.3 FIT vs SEG Comparison Calculator

**Search Intent:** "Should I switch from FIT to SEG?" / "FIT vs SEG comparison"  
**Estimated Monthly UK Search Volume:** 260-480 (small but evergreen, legacy audience)

#### Key Data
- FIT closed to new applicants March 2019
- Existing FIT customers: generation payments 10-15p/kWh + export 5p/kWh (deemed)
- FIT is index-linked (RPI), guaranteed for 20 years
- SEG: export-only, 4-12p/kWh, no inflation protection, no contract guarantee
- **Cannot be on FIT AND SEG simultaneously** for the same system
- Switching FIT → SEG is **usually a bad deal** for existing FIT recipients
- Edge case: if FIT export rate is very low (e.g., deemed export at 5p) and you export a lot, SEG at 12p could be better

#### Top Competitors
- **SunSave** — guide on FIT vs SEG switching
- **Uswitch** — SEG vs FIT comparison guide
- **CalculatorCity** — UK solar FIT calculator
- **Great Home** — mentions FIT in SEG calculator

#### Market Gap 💡
- **No dedicated FIT vs SEG comparison calculator** — it's always a paragraph in a guide
- This is a **simple calculation**: enter FIT generation rate, FIT export rate, annual generation, compare with SEG rate
- Small audience but **high-intent** — these people are making a financial decision worth £thousands
- Evergreen content — FIT recipients will exist until 2039

### 4.3 Grid Independence Calculator

**Search Intent:** "How independent can I be from the grid?" / "off grid calculator uk"

- **Grid independence %** = (solar generation used in home) / (total home consumption) × 100

## 7. UK-Specific Reference Data

### Electricity Prices (Ofgem Price Cap, April 2026)
- Standard variable rate: ~24.5p/kWh (varies by region, £1-3p either way)
- Standing charge: ~28-32p/day
- Off-peak (Economy 7): ~10-14p/kWh
- Octopus Go overnight: ~7-9p/kWh

### Solar Panel Costs (2026)
- 4kWp system: £5,000-£7,000 (installed, including 0% VAT)
- 6kWp system: £7,000-£10,000
- 8-10kWp system: £9,000-£14,000
- Per panel (400W): ~£350-£500 (supply only)
- Typical panel: 400-450W, dimensions ~1.7m × 1.0m

### Battery Costs (2026)
- 5-7 kWh: £3,000-£5,000 (installed)
- 10-13.5 kWh: £5,000-£8,000 (Tesla Powerwall 2 ~£8,000)
- 15-20 kWh: £7,000-£12,000
- VAT: 0% with solar, 5% standalone
- Lifespan: 10-15 years (6,000-10,000 cycles)
- Round-trip efficiency: 90-95%

### UK Household Electricity Usage
- Small flat/1-bed: 1,800-2,500 kWh/year
- 2-3 bed house: 2,700-3,600 kWh/year  
- 4+ bed house: 3,500-4,500 kWh/year
- With EV: add 2,000-3,000 kWh/year
- With heat pump: add 4,000-8,000 kWh/year
- Average daily: 7-12 kWh

### Self-Consumption Ratios (UK)
- No battery, home all day: 40-55%
- No battery, out at work: 25-35%
- With battery, home all day: 75-90%
- With battery, out at work: 65-80%
- With battery + EV charging in daytime: 80-95%

### SEG Rates Summary (April 2026)
- Best fixed: Octopus / Ovo at 12p/kWh
- Worst major: Shell at 4p/kWh
- Variable (Flux export): 5-25p/kWh depending on time
- Agile export: varies half-hourly, can be negative or very high

### VAT Rules
- Solar panels + battery installed together: **0% VAT**
- Solar panels alone: **5% VAT**
- Battery alone: **5% VAT** (changed from 20% in 2024)
- This is a **key selling point** for getting both together

### MCS Certification
- Required for SEG eligibility
- MCS = Microgeneration Certification Scheme
- All reputable installers are MCS-certified
- Without MCS: cannot claim SEG payments
- Certificate provided by installer after installation

## 8. Competitive Differentiation Strategy

### What Thinker Toolbox Should Do Differently

1. **No lead generation, no sign-up, no "get a quote"** — every competitor is a lead-gen funnel. We're the honest calculator.

2. **Show the math** — display the calculation breakdown so users trust the results. "Your savings = 1,600 kWh × 24.5p = £392 + 2,400 kWh × 12p = £288 = £680/year"

3. **Monthly breakdowns** — show savings by month, not just annual. The summer/winter variation is the #1 thing people don't understand about solar.

4. **Compare scenarios** — "with battery vs without", "Octopus Flux vs Go", "south-facing vs east-west". Side-by-side comparison is powerful.

5. **UK region selector** — not just "UK average". Let people pick their region for accurate generation estimates.

6. **Tariff integration** — Octopus tariffs specifically. 4M+ UK customers, huge overlap with solar owners.

7. **Battery optimisation** — the single biggest gap. Charge from grid at 7p, export solar at 22p. Show the arbitrage.

8. **Visual charts** — monthly generation chart, monthly savings chart, cumulative payback chart. Charts share well on social media.

9. **Works offline** — Thinker Toolbox is a PWA. Solar calculators should work offline too (important for field use).

10. **Mobile-first** — most people research solar on their phone. Our calculators are already mobile-optimised.
