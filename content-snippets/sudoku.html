How to Use This Sudoku Solver

  1. Enter your puzzle — Type the numbers from your Sudoku puzzle into the grid. Use the number keys 1–9 for given digits; leave empty cells blank.
  2. Choose the grid size — Supports 4×4, 9×9, and 16×16 puzzles.
  3. Hit Solve — The solver finds the solution using constraint propagation and backtracking. Most 9×9 puzzles solve instantly.
  4. View the solution — The solved numbers appear in a different colour so you can see which cells were filled in.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can every Sudoku be solved?

Only valid Sudokus have a unique solution. If you enter an invalid puzzle (contradictory clues), the solver will tell you it can't be solved. If a puzzle has multiple solutions, the solver will find one of them and indicate that it's not unique.

What's the difference between 4×4, 9×9, and 16×16?

4×4 uses numbers 1–4 in 2×2 boxes — good for beginners and children. 9×9 is the standard puzzle with numbers 1–9 in 3×3 boxes. 16×16 uses 0–9 and A–F in 4×4 boxes — much harder and more time-consuming to solve by hand.

Does the solver work offline?

Yes — once you've visited the page, it works offline. The solving algorithm runs entirely in your browser with no server needed. Handy for solving puzzles on a train or plane.

Is my data private?

Yes — the solver runs entirely in your browser. Your puzzle data never leaves your device.
